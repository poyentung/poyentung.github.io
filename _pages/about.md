I’m an ML researcher at [MatNex](https://www.matnex.ai), working on generative models and RL for materials discovery. The approach uses RL fine-tuning to steer a generative model beyond known chemistry, raising the average predicted superconducting temperature from 10 K to 17 K with a stable, unique, and novel rate of 55%+ and over 90% of candidates genuinely outside the known distribution, validated with quantum-mechanical calculations, a Spotlight talk and panel participant at the [ICLR AI4Mat Workshop (2026)](https://sites.google.com/view/ai4mat/schedule).

The bet is that RL gives distributional control that CFG and conditioning don’t, but novel structures are only useful if you can validate them. I also help build equivariant surrogate models (energy, force, stress) to make that loop fast enough to be practical, and am exploring how agentic systems could connect simulation feedback end-to-end, with a longer-term goal of building systems that interface directly with experimental workflows.

Before MatNex I was a postdoc at [Cambridge](https://www.cam.ac.uk) (2021–2024), where I co-developed [DANTE](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43588-025-00760-8), a high-dimensional optimisation framework (2,000D+) published in [Nature Computational Science (2025)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43588-025-00760-8), and led the ML pipeline behind 2 Invar alloys discovered in 3 months, published in [Science (2022)](https://www.science.org/doi/full/10.1126/science.abo4940). PhD in Materials Science at the [Max Planck Institute for Sustainable Materials](https://www.mpie.de/en), with [Prof. Dirk Raabe](https://www.mpie.de/person/43164/2763408).

**Research interests:** diffusion models · RL for generative model steering · agentic systems · closed-loop discovery · OOD evaluation
